High Point University Discovery Pathways

This section shows how students discover HPU and what drives their enrollment decisions, based on data collected through the Office of Accessibility Resources and Services’ Accommodate registration form.

How Students Discovered HPU

Personal connections and trusted relationships dominate initial awareness.

Family, Friends, & Personal Networks

35.5% (139)

Students mostly discovered HPU through personal connections rather than formal marketing.

General Awareness

29.2% (114)

Many students reported learning about HPU indirectly, often saying they had “always known” about it.

School-Based Exposure

16.4% (64)

Students learned about HPU through school-based channels such as counselors, teachers, visits, and presentations.

Online Search and HPU Website

6.6% (26)

Some students discovered HPU online through searches or browsing, reflecting self-directed exploration.

Social Media

6.6% (26)

Some students discovered HPU via social media and digital channels, often through visual or informal content rather than ads.

Campus Visits and Tours

3.6% (14)

A smaller group of students identified campus tours, open houses, or prior visits as their first exposure to HPU.

Athletics and Recruitment

2% (8)

Athletics-related discovery included contact with coaches or recruitment through sports programs.

Key Insight

For student discovery of HPU, the most influential factors were personal connections such as family members, friends, and trusted community relationships, followed by school-based exposure and informal outreach. Digital channels played a secondary but meaningful role, while campus visits and athletics represented smaller, more targeted discovery pathways.

Why Students Choose HPU

For student decision-making, the data strongly indicates that students choose HPU primarily because of how the institution made them feel. A welcoming, inclusive, and “home-like” campus experience emerged as the dominant theme, closely followed by perceptions of individualized support, small class environments, and faculty accessibility. Academic programs and institutional resources further reinforced enrollment decisions, while location and safety appeared less frequently as primary drivers.

Multiple Factors Shaping Enrollment Choices

34.5% (135)

Many students described their enrollment decision as shaped by multiple, interconnected influences, reflecting a holistic sense of fit across experiences.

Campus Environment and Sense of Belonging

25.1% (98)

Feelings of welcome, inclusion, and safety were key in students’ enrollment decisions, with many describing the campus as “felt like home” or “a safe place.”

Small Class Size and Personalized Support

22% (86)

Individualized attention and accessible faculty drew students to HPU’s supportive, personal environment.

Academic Programs & Institutional Offerings

13.6% (53)

Students’ enrollment was strongly influenced by alignment between their goals and HPU’s programs.

Institutional Resources and Support Services

2.8% (11)

Some students cited institutional resources, like accessibility and support services, as influencing their decision.

Location

1.3% (5)

A small number of students identified geographic proximity or location as a determining factor.

Safety

0.08% (3)

Although HPU offers strong safety measures, students rarely cited safety as a key factor in enrollment.

Critical Finding

In summary, student responses demonstrate that High Point University is most commonly discovered through trusted relationships and chosen because of how the institution makes students feel once engaged. The prominence of themes related to belonging, support, and personal attention reflects a clear institutional identity as experienced by students and offers valuable insight into enrollment decision pathways and student engagement priorities.
